Drawing a Dealing Range 9 rules · the process is sequential — every rule must be satisfied before the DR is valid
PROCESS — IN ORDER
1Identify the most recent significant swing in HTF structure (W1 → D1 → H4 → H1 → M15) 2Confirm the swing is structural — displacement + FVG or BOS/CHoCH event, not a minor pivot 3Anchor both boundaries to candle bodies — extend to wicks only for confirmed liquidity sweeps 4Confirm one boundary has been validated by a sweep + return (liquidity event) 5Mark EQ (50%), 61.8%, and 75% levels — draw Fib from wick-to-wick for precision 6Assign direction (bull/bear) based on which BOS or MSS opened the range 7Assign phase (Con → Man → Exp → Ret) based on where price currently is in delivery 8Do not redraw until a full candle body closes beyond a boundary AND price accepts outside 9When redrawing: anchor the new range to the swing that produced the invalidating BOS
R1
Start on HTF — W1, then D1, then H4, then H1, then M15. Never draw a DR on M5 or M1 as the primary range The governing DR is always the highest timeframe with a clear recent swing. M5/M1 can reveal the SMR sub-range formed by the execution CISD, but that is not the governing DR — it is the entry precision tool. Drawing your primary range on M5 causes constant redrawing and destroys the framework's stability.
Hard Rule
R2
Both swing points must be confirmed by structural displacement — a strong impulsive move, FVG, or BOS/CHoCH event. Minor pivots are invalid A swing high or low becomes valid when price retraces from it and breaks the inducement level (the first pullback from the extreme). This break — the inducement break — confirms the swing. If there is no displacement, no FVG, and no BOS/CHoCH, the point is a minor pivot and cannot anchor a DR. A valid swing always has a reason — it participated in a structural event.
Hard Rule
R3
Anchor the boundaries to candle bodies as the default. Extend to wicks only when the wick represents a confirmed liquidity sweep Candle bodies represent where buyers and sellers agreed on price — the open and close. Wicks are liquidity grabs: algorithmic spikes to collect stops above highs or below lows. Anchoring to wick extremes shifts the EQ, distorts the premium/discount zones, and misplaces the 61.8% and 75% levels. Use the wick as the boundary only when it is the obvious sweep candle in the context of the narrative (e.g. the 85% manipulation wick you would not cut).
Hard Rule
R4
For OTE / Fibonacci levels (61.8%, 75%), draw from wick-to-wick — the true extremity of the range This is the one exception where wicks override bodies. When drawing the Fibonacci retracement to find the OTE zone, anchor to the absolute high wick and absolute low wick of the swing. This captures the full institutional range and ensures the 61.8–79% OTE zone is accurate. The boundary drawing (Rule 3) uses bodies; the Fibonacci measurement uses wicks. Both can co-exist on the same range.
Informational
R5
The range opens when a sweep + MSS is confirmed. The sweep sets one boundary; the MSS sets the direction A dealing range does not open arbitrarily. It opens when: (1) price sweeps a prior swing high or low — a liquidity event, and (2) a Market Structure Shift (candle close beyond the opposing swing) confirms the direction of the new range. The sweep candle defines one DR boundary. The MSS candle defines the direction. Without both, you have a price range, not a dealing range.
Hard Rule
R6
Assign direction from the MSS that opened the range — not from bias, not from HTF trend, not from prior structure alone The direction of a DR is determined by which way the MSS broke. A bearish DR opened when price swept a high and the MSS closed below a prior swing low. A bullish DR opened when price swept a low and the MSS closed above a prior swing high. If you cannot point to the specific MSS candle that opened the range, you have not confirmed the direction.
Hard Rule
R7
A CHoCH alone does not reset the dealing range — only a BOS that confirms a new market leg resets it A Change of Character (CHoCH) is the first MSS that breaks prior structure — it is an early reversal signal. It does not invalidate the governing DR on its own. A Break of Structure (BOS) on the governing timeframe, where the new leg is clearly displacing and building value in the new direction, is what resets the range. CHoCH = watch. BOS = redraw.
Hard Rule
R8
Do not redraw the DR from a wick pierce of the boundary — only a full candle body close beyond the boundary initiates the invalidation process A wick beyond the DR High or Low is a liquidity grab — the manipulation phase of the range. It is the most predictable and tradeable event in the framework. Redrawing on a wick means you have missed the setup the range was designed to produce. The DR boundary remains valid until a full candle body closes beyond it. Even then, see Rule 9 — acceptance must be confirmed before redrawing.
Hard Rule
R9
When redrawing: anchor the new range to the swing produced by the invalidating BOS — the old boundary becomes a reference level, not a new anchor Once a DR is invalidated (full body close + acceptance beyond boundary + HTF BOS cascade shift), identify the most recent swing that formed as price broke out. That swing is the new DR anchor. The previous DR High becomes a reference level — buy-side liquidity if it is now below price in a bull structure, sell-side liquidity if it is above in a bear structure. Never draw the new DR using the same H/L as the old one.
Hard Rule

1
Buy from Discount only — never at Premium Bullish bias while the Operative DR price is above 61.8% (Premium zone) fires a Zone Conflict warning in the Smart Bias Compiler. Institutional logic: premium is where smart money distributes, not accumulates.
Hard Rule
2
Sell from Premium only — never at Discount Bearish bias while the Operative DR price is below 38.2% (Discount zone) fires a Zone Conflict warning. Discount is where smart money accumulates, not distributes.
Hard Rule
3
DR hierarchy governs all other context PDAs, session liquidity, and BOS are precision tools — not bias tools. A PDA in the wrong DR zone is invalid regardless of type, timeframe, or overlay confluence. The DR is the foundational filter and governs everything beneath it.
Hard Rule
4
Zone-opposed PDAs are excluded from bias compilation Any PDA whose direction opposes its DR zone (bull PDA at Premium, bear PDA at Discount) receives a ⊘ Zone Invalid badge, is excluded from Aligned/Conflict counters, and is invisible to the Smart Bias Compiler signal tile.

Retracement Trade Rules 4 rules
7
Retracement setup only available when Operative DR phase = Retracement (↩) The Retracement button in the Setup tab is gated — it dims and explains why if the DR is in Consolidation, Manipulation, or Expansion phase. It activates automatically once the correct phase is set.
Gate
8
Retracement blocked when governing HTF DR is in opposing expansion If the HTF DR above the Operative DR is expanding in the opposite direction, counter-trend risk is too high. E.g. D1 bearish expansion while attempting a bullish H4 retracement — gated with reason displayed.
Gate
9
Retracement target is EQ only — never beyond the 50% level When Retracement is the active setup type, TP1 and TP2 warn if price exceeds the DR EQ. The trade is a counter-DR delivery — targeting beyond EQ means trading against the governing DR direction.
Hard Rule
10
Counter-direction entry during Expansion — informational phase note If the DR is in Expansion and trader verdict is counter-direction, the SBC Phase Note surfaces: retracement trade, EQ target only. Not a block — awareness that the governing DR is delivering against the trade.
Informational
Manipulation Phase Rules 1 rule
11
No bias commitment during Manipulation phase — wait for CISD + MSS When the Operative DR is in Manipulation (⚡) phase, the SBC Phase Note fires regardless of trade direction. The Manipulation phase is the liquidity sweep — directional bias cannot be confirmed until CISD (close past opposing OB) and MSS (candle close beyond swing) are both confirmed.
Informational
When to Invalidate a Dealing Range 5 rules · use the DR Validity Checker in Setup tab
12
75%+ retracement with opposing CISD/FVG inside the range is NOT invalidation — it is the OTE zone For a bearish DR, the Optimal Trade Entry sits between 61.8%–79% premium. Price retracing to this depth with opposing bullish signals is the manipulation leg collecting buy-side liquidity. The DR remains valid. The bullish FVG at 75% is your bearish entry PDA — it just flipped to a supply zone. Wait for a bearish CISD confirming re-entry into expansion.
Informational
13
DR is structurally intact until price closes beyond the boundary with a full candle body A wick pierce beyond the DR high/low is a liquidity sweep — not invalidation. Only a full candle body close beyond the boundary begins the invalidation process. This is the first gate in the DR Validity Checker.
Hard Rule
14
A body close beyond the boundary is only invalidating if price accepts — holds outside and builds structure If price closes beyond the DR high/low but immediately returns inside on the next candle, that is a false break — the DR may still be valid. Acceptance means price consolidates or builds higher lows (bull) / lower highs (bear) outside the boundary. Only then is the DR definitively broken.
Hard Rule
15
LTF opposing CISD inside the range = manipulation signal. HTF opposing CISD = structural shift signal requiring BOS check A bullish CISD on M15/H1 inside a bearish DR is almost certainly the manipulation leg exhausting — stay bearish. A bullish CISD on H4 or D1 is a different signal: it indicates a potential structural shift. In this case proceed to the HTF BOS cascade check before deciding.
Hard Rule
16
HTF BOS cascade opposing the DR direction is the final invalidation signal — redraw from new swing If the HTF BOS cascade has shifted against the current DR direction (e.g. D1 and H4 now bullish while you are trading a bearish H4 DR), the DR thesis is compromised. Do not trade it. Redraw the dealing range from the new most-recent swing high to its subsequent swing low. The old DR high becomes part of the new bullish range's premium zone.
Hard Rule
IRL Exhaustion Setup bearish DR · 75%+ retracement · H4 governing · H1/M15 execution
CONCEPT
A bearish Dealing Range retraces to the 61.8–85% premium (OTE zone). A bullish FVG forms during that retracement. That FVG is Internal Range Liquidity (IRL) — it exists to be consumed as the manipulation leg collects buy-side stops above 75%. The DR remains fully bearish. Once the IRL FVG is filled, the manipulation is complete and the expansion leg down can begin.

The deeper the retracement (85%+ > 75%), the more buy-side liquidity has been collected, and the higher the probability of the subsequent expansion. An 85% retracement is not a warning — it is confirmation the manipulation was thorough.
CANONICAL EXAMPLE — H4 DR · H1 EXECUTION
ContextH4 bearish DR confirmed. Price retraces to 85% premium. D1/W1 BOS bearish or neutral. SignalH1 bearish CISD forms. Before the CISD, an H1 IFVG is present — the inverted bullish IRL gap. Path ASell limit at 50% of H1 IFVG (CE). SL above the 85% sweep wick high. TP1 = H4 DR EQ. TP2 = H4 DR Low. Path BDrop to M15. Grade the bearish DR formed by the H1 CISD. Sell limit at M15 OTE (61.8–75%). SL above M15 DR High. Same TP1/TP2 as Path A. AuthorityH4 DR governs both paths. If price closes above the 85% wick on H4, both setups invalidate simultaneously.
17
The bullish FVG formed during a 75%+ retracement in a bearish DR is IRL — a target to be consumed, not a reversal signal Internal Range Liquidity is the draw-on-liquidity that the manipulation leg is reaching for. The bullish FVG at 75–85% premium exists because price needed to fill it to fuel the next leg down. The DR is fully valid. Deeper retracement = more buy-side stops collected = higher probability expansion. An 85% retracement is confirmation, not a red flag.
Informational
18
Primary entry trigger: bearish CISD on H1 (or M15) after the IRL FVG is consumed Once price fills the bullish IRL FVG, wait for a bearish CISD on H1 — a candle close below the open of the last bullish delivery candle. This is the state-of-delivery flip: the market was delivering bullishly to fill IRL, now it has reversed. The CISD is required — do not enter before it is confirmed on the close.
Hard Rule
19
Path A — H1 IFVG entry: sell limit at 50% of the inverted gap (consequent encroachment) The H1 IFVG that forms before or during the bearish CISD is the precision entry zone. Place a sell limit at its 50% midpoint. If price does not retrace into the IFVG after the CISD, take market entry on the CISD candle close. If price closes above the top of the IFVG, the entry is invalid — do not chase.
Gate
20
Path B — M15 DR OTE entry: grade the M15 DR formed by the H1 CISD, enter at 61.8–75% premium The H1 bearish CISD defines a new M15 dealing range — the sweep + MSS on M15 sets the H/L. Grade that M15 DR and place a sell limit at its OTE zone (61.8–75% premium). SL above the M15 DR High (tighter than Path A). Carry runners from M15 DR Low → H4 DR EQ → H4 DR Low. Path B gives tighter SL and higher RR but requires more granular management.
Gate
21
SL: above the manipulation wick high — the candle that swept 75%+ buy-side liquidity Stop loss goes above the wick of the specific candle that swept buy-side at 75–85% premium. Not above the DR High — above that wick. If price closes above it, the bearish DR thesis is wrong and requires a redraw. This structurally tight SL is what produces the 3:1–5:1 RR typical of this setup. Never widen the SL to the DR High to avoid being stopped out.
Hard Rule
22
TP sequence: DR EQ (IRL — partials + move SL to BE), then DR Low (ERL — full target) TP1 = H4 DR EQ (50%). This is the first significant IRL target on the way down — take partials here and move SL to BE. The BE rule applies: never move SL before TP1 is confirmed. TP2 = H4 DR Low (External Range Liquidity — sell-side stops below the range). This is the delivery target the DR was built toward. Hold runners for the full expansion.
Hard Rule
!
HTF override: do not take this setup against a live D1 or W1 bullish expansion If D1 or W1 is in active bullish expansion, the H4 bearish DR is counter-trend. ICT would either skip entirely or treat it as a scalp with TP1 = DR EQ only — no runners to DR Low. The HTF cascade is the final authority. Confirm D1/W1 BOS is bearish or neutral before committing to the full TP2 target.
Hard Rule
