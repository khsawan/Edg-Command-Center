Governing DR subsection of Dealing Range · R1 · source: ICT Mentorship 2022
DEFINITION
The governing DR is the dealing range on the timeframe immediately above your execution timeframe that provides two filters: direction (bull or bear) and location (premium or discount). Both filters must be satisfied before any entry on the lower timeframe is valid. The governing DR is not traded directly — it is the authority that sanctions or vetoes every setup below it.

G1
The governing DR is always at least one timeframe above the execution timeframe — you never govern and execute on the same timeframe Governing and executing on the same timeframe removes the directional filter entirely — you are trading noise, not structure. The separation between governing and execution is what creates the edge. The governing DR answers what and where. The execution timeframe answers when and how.

G2

Bearish governing DR = only short setups on the execution timeframe. Bullish governing DR = only long setups. Location: only enter from the correct zone of the governing DR. Bearish governing DR = entries only from premium (above EQ). Bullish governing DR = entries only from discount (below EQ). A setup with correct direction but wrong location is invalid. A setup with correct location but wrong direction is invalid. Both filters must pass simultaneously.
Hard Rule

That SMR sub-range is not graded, not assigned a phase, and not used for bias. It exists only to find the limit order placement. When the governing DR is M15, M5 is the floor — nothing below M5 has structural meaning within this framework.
Hard Rule
G4
The governing DR is relative — it shifts depending on where you are in the hierarchy. It is not a fixed timeframe If you are executing on M15, the governing DR is H1 or H4. If you are executing on H1, the governing DR is H4 or D1. If you are executing on H4, the governing DR is D1 or W1. The governing DR is always the most recently confirmed DR on the timeframe directly above your execution level. If no confirmed DR exists on that timeframe, move up one level until you find one. If no DR is confirmed on any timeframe above your execution level — you have no governing context and no valid setup exists.

You do not wait for price to retrace back below 79% — that retrace may never come and waiting for it invents a rule that does not exist in the framework. Enter from the best available PDA above EQ, wherever it sits. The 79% is a reference. The PDA with confirmation is the trigger. SL always goes above the manipulation wick — not the DR High body. IMPORTANT — DR NON-CONTINUITY
When a DR is invalidated or completes its delivery, a new DR does not open automatically. There is no mechanical handoff. The market enters a structural no-man's land — no governing range, no direction filter, no valid setup. Sit on your hands until the full confirmation sequence (sweep + CHoCH + BOS) opens a new range from scratch.
I1
A wick beyond the DR boundary is never invalidation — it is the manipulation phase and is the most tradeable event in the framework Wicks beyond the DR High or Low are algorithmic liquidity grabs — the market reaching above or below the boundary to collect stop orders before reversing. Redrawing on a wick means you have misread the manipulation phase as a breakout and missed the setup the range was built to produce. A wick, regardless of how far it extends, does not invalidate the DR. This is the most common mistake in the framework and the one that causes the most missed setups.
Hard Rule
I2
A full candle body close beyond the boundary initiates the invalidation process — it does not confirm it The body close is Gate 1. It tells you the market may be accepting outside the range. You do not redraw at this point. You watch whether price accepts at the new level or returns inside. If price returns inside on the next candle — the body close was a false break. The DR is not only still valid, it is reinforced. A failed invalidation attempt is one of the strongest confirmations that the boundary is genuine.
Hard Rule
I3
Price acceptance outside the boundary is Gate 2 — follow-through candles closing beyond the boundary confirm the market is not rejecting the level Acceptance means at least one follow-through candle opens and closes beyond the boundary without returning inside. This distinguishes a genuine break from a false break. Acceptance alone does not invalidate the DR — it opens the alert state. You are now watching for the CHoCH and BOS that will formally close the range.
Hard Rule
I4
A CHoCH on the governing timeframe after acceptance is the first structural reversal signal — the DR is wounded but not yet invalidated The CHoCH confirms the first break of prior structure in the opposing direction. It is the warning signal — not the final invalidation. The DR is under serious threat at this point. You should not be entering new positions in the original DR direction. But you do not redraw until the BOS confirms.
Hard Rule
I5
A BOS on the governing timeframe is the definitive invalidation event — redraw immediately, old boundaries become reference levels only The BOS confirms a new structural leg has begun in the opposing direction. The old DR is formally invalidated. The old DR High or Low does not disappear — it becomes a reference level: buy-side liquidity if now below price in a bullish structure, sell-side liquidity if above price in a bearish structure. Never use the old boundaries as anchors for the new DR. The new DR anchors to the swing produced by the invalidating BOS.
Hard Rule
I6
HTF BOS cascade is the final and strongest invalidation signal — when the timeframe above confirms the opposing BOS, the invalidation is complete across all levels When the governing timeframe BOS is confirmed by an opposing BOS on the timeframe above it, the structural shift is complete at multiple timeframe levels simultaneously. This is the point of no return — the old DR framework is gone on all timeframes. The cascade also signals that the new opposing DR will likely be stronger and more sustained than a single-timeframe invalidation, because the HTF is now aligned with the new direction.

Dealing Range 9 rules · the process is sequential — every rule must be satisfied before the DR is valid
PROCESS — IN ORDER
1Identify the most recent significant swing in HTF structure (W1 → D1 → H4 → H1 → M15) 2Confirm the swing is structural — displacement + FVG or BOS/CHoCH event, not a minor pivot 3Anchor both boundaries to candle bodies — extend to wicks only for confirmed liquidity sweeps 4Confirm one boundary has been validated by a sweep + return (liquidity event) 5Mark EQ (50%), 61.8%, and 75% levels — draw Fib from wick-to-wick for precision 6Assign direction (bull/bear) based on which BOS or MSS opened the range 7Assign phase (Con → Man → Exp → Ret) based on where price currently is in delivery 8Do not redraw until a full candle body closes beyond a boundary AND price accepts outside 9When redrawing: anchor the new range to the swing that produced the invalidating BOS
R1
Start on HTF — W1, then D1, then H4, then H1, then M15. Never draw a DR on M5 or M1 as the primary range The governing DR is always the highest timeframe with a clear recent swing. M5/M1 can reveal the SMR sub-range formed by the execution CISD, but that is not the governing DR — it is the entry precision tool. Drawing your primary range on M5 causes constant redrawing and destroys the framework's stability. → See Governing DR rules
Hard Rule
R2
Both swing points must be confirmed by structural displacement — a strong impulsive move, FVG, or BOS/CHoCH event. Minor pivots are invalid A swing high or low becomes valid when price retraces from it and breaks the inducement level (the first pullback from the extreme). This break — the inducement break — confirms the swing. If there is no displacement, no FVG, and no BOS/CHoCH, the point is a minor pivot and cannot anchor a DR. A valid swing always has a reason — it participated in a structural event.
Hard Rule
R3
Anchor the boundaries to candle bodies as the default. Extend to wicks only when the wick represents a confirmed liquidity sweep Candle bodies represent where buyers and sellers agreed on price — the open and close. Wicks are liquidity grabs: algorithmic spikes to collect stops above highs or below lows. Anchoring to wick extremes shifts the EQ, distorts the premium/discount zones, and misplaces the 61.8% and 75% levels. Use the wick as the boundary only when it is the obvious sweep candle in the context of the narrative (e.g. the 85% manipulation wick you would not cut).
Hard Rule
R4
For OTE / Fibonacci levels (61.8%, 75%), draw from wick-to-wick — the true extremity of the range This is the one exception where wicks override bodies. When drawing the Fibonacci retracement to find the OTE zone, anchor to the absolute high wick and absolute low wick of the swing. This captures the full institutional range and ensures the 61.8–79% OTE zone is accurate. The boundary drawing (Rule 3) uses bodies; the Fibonacci measurement uses wicks. Both can co-exist on the same range.
Informational
R5
The range opens when a sweep + MSS is confirmed. The sweep sets one boundary; the MSS sets the direction A dealing range does not open arbitrarily. It opens when: (1) price sweeps a prior swing high or low — a liquidity event, and (2) a Market Structure Shift (candle close beyond the opposing swing) confirms the direction of the new range. The sweep candle defines one DR boundary. The MSS candle defines the direction. Without both, you have a price range, not a dealing range.
Hard Rule
R6
Assign direction from the MSS that opened the range — not from bias, not from HTF trend, not from prior structure alone The direction of a DR is determined by which way the MSS broke. A bearish DR opened when price swept a high and the MSS closed below a prior swing low. A bullish DR opened when price swept a low and the MSS closed above a prior swing high. If you cannot point to the specific MSS candle that opened the range, you have not confirmed the direction.
Hard Rule
R7
A CHoCH alone does not reset the dealing range — only a BOS that confirms a new market leg resets it A Change of Character (CHoCH) is the first MSS that breaks prior structure — it is an early reversal signal. It does not invalidate the governing DR on its own. A Break of Structure (BOS) on the governing timeframe, where the new leg is clearly displacing and building value in the new direction, is what resets the range. CHoCH = watch. BOS = redraw.
Hard Rule
R8
Do not redraw the DR from a wick pierce of the boundary — only a full candle body close beyond the boundary initiates the invalidation process A wick beyond the DR High or Low is a liquidity grab — the manipulation phase of the range. It is the most predictable and tradeable event in the framework. Redrawing on a wick means you have missed the setup the range was designed to produce. The DR boundary remains valid until a full candle body closes beyond it. Even then, see Rule 9 — acceptance must be confirmed before redrawing. → See full invalidation criteria
Hard Rule
R9
When redrawing: anchor the new range to the swing produced by the invalidating BOS — the old boundary becomes a reference level, not a new anchor Once a DR is invalidated (full body close + acceptance beyond boundary + HTF BOS cascade shift), identify the most recent swing that formed as price broke out. That swing is the new DR anchor. The previous DR High becomes a reference level — buy-side liquidity if it is now below price in a bull structure, sell-side liquidity if it is above in a bear structure. Never draw the new DR using the same H/L as the old one.

Reversal — Aligned Setups · bearish DR · sweep of DR High → entry → DR Low · direction aligned with governing DR
CONCEPT
In a bearish DR, price sweeps the DR High — collecting buy-side stops above the upper boundary. The algorithm has completed the manipulation phase. A bearish CISD confirms the state of delivery has flipped. The expansion leg toward the DR Low begins. This is the highest probability setup in the framework because it is fully aligned with the governing DR direction, the HTF draw, and the structural sequence the range was built to produce.

PDA Types & Hierarchy subsection of PDAs · FVG is the primary tool — OB and BRK require FVG confluence to be valid
FVG — FAIR VALUE GAP
P1
Three candle imbalance — candle 1 high does not overlap candle 3 low (bearish) or candle 1 low does not overlap candle 3 high (bullish) The FVG is the space between candle 1 and candle 3 that candle 2 — the displacement candle — created by moving too aggressively for the market to fill. This imbalance is the institutional footprint. The gap itself is the zone price will return to fill.
Hard Rule
P2
Valid FVG requires three conditions simultaneously — displacement candle origin, correct DR zone, unmitigated All three must be true. A FVG formed by a non-displacement candle is noise. A FVG in the wrong DR zone (bearish FVG in discount zone of a bearish DR) is invalid — price is not expected to react there. A mitigated FVG has been consumed — it no longer exists as an entry zone.
Hard Rule
P3
One touch = fully mitigated — the FVG is consumed the moment price trades into it There is no partial mitigation rule. There is no 50% CE mitigation rule. The instant price reaches the FVG boundary, the PDA is consumed. Do not re-enter from a mitigated FVG. Strike it from the chart and move to the next unmitigated zone.
Hard Rule
IFVG — INVERTED FAIR VALUE GAP
P4
IFVG is a FVG that price has fully traded through — it becomes a zone of opposing reaction When price trades through a FVG entirely, the imbalance flips. A bearish FVG that price trades above becomes a bullish IFVG — former resistance becomes support. A bullish FVG that price trades below becomes a bearish IFVG — former support becomes resistance. The IFVG is treated as a valid entry zone in the same way as a fresh FVG.
Hard Rule
P5
IFVG is the first signal of a shift in momentum — it precedes structural confirmation The moment a FVG is traded through, momentum has shifted against the original delivery. This is the earliest warning available — earlier than a CHoCH, earlier than a BOS. The IFVG does not trigger an entry alone but it alerts you that the current PDA is no longer holding and a directional change may be forming. Watch for confirmation on the LTF.
Informational
BPR — BALANCED PRICE RANGE
P6
BPR is the overlap zone of a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG at the same price level — highest confluence entry zone When a bullish FVG and a bearish FVG exist at the same price level, their overlap creates the BPR. This is not an OB overlap. This is not a BRK overlap. It is exclusively two opposing FVGs intersecting. The overlap zone represents two institutional imbalances requiring fill at the same location — maximum confluence. The BPR is the highest priority entry zone when it exists in the correct DR zone.
Hard Rule
P7
BPR entry is placed at the first boundary of the overlap zone — not the midpoint, not the far boundary When price enters the BPR overlap zone, the first boundary it reaches is the entry. Price touching the first boundary of the two overlapping FVGs is sufficient — both imbalances begin being addressed at that level. Do not wait for 50% CE of the overlap. Do not wait for the far boundary. First touch of the overlap = entry trigger.
Hard Rule
OB — ORDER BLOCK
P8
OB is the last opposing candle — or uninterrupted succeeding candles in the same direction — immediately before a displacement move The OB is where institutional orders were placed that caused the displacement. It is the last candle in the opposing direction before the move — or a series of consecutive candles moving in the same direction as the OB without interruption, immediately before displacement. One candle breaking the sequence invalidates the OB candidate. The displacement candle that follows must be clear and decisive.
Hard Rule
P9
OB is only valid when it overlaps a FVG — OB alone is not a valid entry zone An OB without FVG overlap is a structural observation, not an entry. The FVG is what confirms the institutional imbalance. When the OB overlaps the FVG, the combination confirms both the order origin and the delivery imbalance — that confluence is the entry zone. Without the FVG, skip the OB entirely.
Hard Rule
P10
OB entry zone — body when wick does not overlap FVG · wick when wick overlaps FVG Case 1: the wick of the OB candle does not reach into the FVG — the entry zone is the body of the OB candle. The body represents the institutional order range. Case 2: the wick of the OB candle extends into the FVG — the entry zone is the wick. The wick is now confluent with the FVG and that is the precise institutional level. Always identify which case applies before placing the limit order.
Hard Rule
BRK — BREAKER BLOCK
P11
BRK is a failed OB — an OB that price has traded through, flipping its delivery direction When price returns to an OB and trades through it entirely, the OB becomes a Breaker Block. A failed bullish OB becomes a bearish BRK — former support becomes resistance. A failed bearish OB becomes a bullish BRK — former resistance becomes support. The BRK represents trapped institutional orders that the algorithm will use as a delivery zone on the return.
Hard Rule
P12
BRK is only valid when it overlaps a FVG — entry zone is the BRK boundary Same principle as the OB — the BRK alone is not sufficient. The FVG overlap is the validity condition. When valid, the entry zone is the BRK boundary itself — not the FVG boundary, not the midpoint of the overlap. The BRK boundary is the institutional level where the trapped orders reside. Place the limit at the BRK boundary when it is inside or aligned with the FVG zone.

Manipulation Phase Setup · the permission slip — no manipulation phase means no entry exists
GOVERNING PRINCIPLE
The manipulation phase is the algorithm collecting liquidity before delivering price in the intended direction. It is the only event that confirms institutional intent. Without it there is no entry — regardless of how clean the structure looks on any timeframe.
MP1
The manipulation phase is a liquidity sweep — price moves beyond a significant level to collect stops, then closes back inside The algorithm reaches above a high or below a low to trigger resting orders — buy stops above highs, sell stops below lows. Once those orders are filled, price reverses. The defining characteristic is that price does not hold beyond the level — it sweeps it and returns. The sweep is the manipulation. The return inside is the confirmation. A body close beyond the level is not a sweep — it is a break, and the setup does not exist.
Hard Rule
MP2
No manipulation phase = no entry — aggressive expansion without a prior sweep is the algorithm delivering, not offering A series of BOS candles with no retrace, no sweep, and no CISD is expansion phase delivery. The algorithm is executing an order it already confirmed elsewhere. There is no entry zone — no FVG from a sweep candle, no sub-DR from a manipulation leg, no structural permission. Entering during expansion without a prior manipulation phase is chasing momentum with no framework backing. Wait for the manipulation phase to form before reading any confirmation sequence.
Hard Rule
SWEEP TYPES — ALL VALID MANIPULATION EVENTS
MP3
Type 1 — Boundary sweep · price sweeps a DR High, DR Low, EQ, prior swing high/low, or session high/low Any significant liquidity level is a valid sweep target — not just DR boundaries. EQ collects resting orders from traders who entered at the midpoint. Prior swing highs and lows collect breakout traders' stops. Session highs and lows collect overnight positions. The level does not need to be a DR boundary to constitute a manipulation phase — it needs to be a level where stops are resting. Price sweeps it, closes back inside, and the manipulation phase is confirmed.
Hard Rule
MP4
Type 2 — Second candle sweep · candle 1 closes at a level, candle 2 sweeps that close and rejects When a candle closes at or near a significant level, its close price becomes a liquidity point — traders place stops just beyond it. The following candle may sweep that close level, triggering those stops, before reversing. This is an internal liquidity grab. The two-candle sequence — close at level, sweep on next candle, rejection — is a valid manipulation phase. It is most significant when it occurs inside or at the boundary of a PDA.
Hard Rule
MP5
Type 3 — Turtle Soup · price rejects from a PDA, returns and sweeps the initial rejection point inside that PDA Price enters a PDA zone and initially rejects — creating a visible swing point. That swing point immediately attracts stop orders from traders who entered on the rejection. Price returns to the PDA, sweeps that initial rejection swing point collecting those stops, then delivers from the PDA in the intended direction. The sweep of the prior rejection point inside the PDA is the manipulation phase. This is the highest confluence sweep type — it occurs inside an already-valid institutional zone and confirms the PDA is holding.
Hard Rule
CONFIRMATION REQUIREMENT
MP6
The sweep alone is never the entry trigger — a lower timeframe confirmation sequence is always required After any sweep type on the governing TF, you drop to the confirmation TF and read structure. The confirmation sequence is CHoCH → retrace → BOS = CISD. The BOS is the entry trigger — not the sweep. The sweep is the permission. The confirmation sequence is the activation. No setup in the framework enters directly from a sweep candle on the governing TF without first confirming the structural shift on the lower TF.
Hard Rule
MP7
The sweep must occur on the governing TF — a lower TF sweep of a higher TF level does not activate the higher TF confirmation sequence A M15 candle sweeping an H1 DR High is not sufficient to govern the H1 confirmation chain. The sweep must be visible and confirmed on the TF it belongs to. An H1 sweep activates the M15 confirmation sequence. A D1 sweep activates the H1 confirmation sequence. A M15 sweep activates the M5 confirmation sequence. The TF hierarchy is non-negotiable — a lower TF sweep is a lower TF event and produces a lower TF entry only.
Hard Rule
MP8
A second sweep of the same level does not reset the sequence — it is still a sweep as long as price does not hold and closes back inside If price sweeps a level, closes back inside, and then sweeps it again on the following candle — both are valid sweep events provided neither candle body closes beyond the level. The second sweep may collect a deeper pool of liquidity and produce a stronger reversal. It does not invalidate the first sweep nor does it restart the confirmation sequence — if the LTF confirmation is already in progress, it continues. If no confirmation has formed yet, the second sweep refreshes the manipulation and the LTF sequence begins from that point.

Retracement Trade Rules 4 rules
7
Retracement setup only available when Operative DR phase = Retracement (↩) The Retracement button in the Setup tab is gated — it dims and explains why if the DR is in Consolidation, Manipulation, or Expansion phase. It activates automatically once the correct phase is set.
Gate
8
Retracement blocked when governing HTF DR is in opposing expansion If the HTF DR above the Operative DR is expanding in the opposite direction, counter-trend risk is too high. E.g. D1 bearish expansion while attempting a bullish H4 retracement — gated with reason displayed.
Gate
9
Retracement target is EQ only — never beyond the 50% level When Retracement is the active setup type, TP1 and TP2 warn if price exceeds the DR EQ. The trade is a counter-DR delivery — targeting beyond EQ means trading against the governing DR direction.
Hard Rule
10
Counter-direction entry during Expansion — informational phase note If the DR is in Expansion and trader verdict is counter-direction, the SBC Phase Note surfaces: retracement trade, EQ target only. Not a block — awareness that the governing DR is delivering against the trade.
